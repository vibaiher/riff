# Free mode

Free is Riff's listening mode. You play, Riff listens, and in real time it tells you what it hears: the exact note, the chord your fingers form, the tempo you're keeping. That's it.

There are no objectives, no validation, no right or wrong. Free doesn't know what you're trying to play and doesn't care. It only reflects back what comes from your hands, with precision and without judgment.

---

## What it shows

As you play, Free displays:

- **Note** — the pitch it's detecting at any given moment
- **Chord** — the chord formed by the notes it hears together
- **Tempo** — the pulse it infers from your playing

The display updates in real time. When you stop playing, it stops updating.

---

## What it's for

Free is the most honest mode in Riff. Many guitarists spend years playing without really knowing what they're doing — what note they're on, what chord they've landed on, whether what they think they're playing is what's actually coming out. Free closes that gap.

It's useful for warming up before a Learn session, exploring a chord or progression you just heard in a song, improvising without structure, or simply playing for the pleasure of it while Riff listens in silence and names what emerges.

It is not a practice tool. It has no memory, no plan, no feedback beyond the present moment. What you do in Free stays in Free.
