# Riff

Riff is an interactive terminal application for learning to play electric guitar. Not an academy, not a method, not a productivity app dressed up as music. It is a digital mentor that listens to you play, learns how you learn, and guides you toward the songs you want to play.

The philosophy is simple: technique exists to serve playing something real. Riff never puts you through abstract exercises — everything you practice has a concrete destination you chose yourself.

---

## Three modes, one experience

Riff has three independent modes, accessible at any time from the main menu.

### Free

Listening mode in real time. You play and Riff listens — no objectives, no validation, no pressure. On screen it shows in real time what it detects: the exact note you're playing, the chord your fingers form, the tempo you're keeping. Nothing more.

It is the most honest of the three modes. It doesn't tell you what to play or how you should do it. It simply reflects back what comes from your hands, with precision and without judgment. That has more value than it seems: many guitarists spend years playing without really knowing what they're doing. Free turns intuition into knowledge.

It serves as a warm-up before a Learn session, a way to explore a chord you just heard in a song, a space to improvise without a net, or simply to play for the pleasure of playing while Riff listens in silence and names what emerges.

### Compose

Creative mode with musical memory. Riff listens to you — live as you play, or from an audio recording you load — and internally stores the notes it detects: their pitch, their duration, their relationship to each other. You don't see that process. You just play.

When you press `G`, Riff analyzes everything it has accumulated and composes a melody that musically accompanies what you played. Not a generic backing track or a pre-recorded loop — something built from your material, respecting your key, your rhythm, your character. Sometimes it sounds exactly as you expected. Sometimes it surprises you.

The result plays back immediately. You can listen to it, repeat it, play over it, or start fresh with another fragment. There is no correct destination — Compose exists for the moment when the music coming from your hands deserves a response.

### Learn

Guided learning mode. The pedagogical core of Riff. It works around a living learning plan organized around real songs you have chosen.

---

## The living learning plan

The first time you enter Learn, Riff asks what you want to achieve. With that information it generates a selection of songs suited to your level — and before you choose, it plays a fragment of each one so you decide with your ears, not just the song title. With your selection it builds a plan: songs ordered as projects, with deliberate practice sessions between each one, calibrated to your available time.

The next times you open Riff, today's session is already prepared. No menus, no decisions, no friction.

At any point you can tell Riff in natural language that you want to adjust the plan — change your goal, add a song, reduce practice time — and it regenerates everything while respecting your history and accumulated progress. Nothing is discarded, everything adapts.

---

## How a learning session works

Every Learn session follows a clear structure.

It starts with a briefing: Riff tells you where you are in the plan, what skill today's session works on and why, and what fragment of the target song you'll play at the end. Never generic — always based on what happened in the previous session.

During the session you work on specific chords, transitions, and techniques. The tab is rendered measure by measure in the terminal. Before you play any fragment, Riff plays it with electric guitar sound so your ear knows where it's going. Then it's your turn. The cursor advances when you hit the right note and waits if you miss — no penalty, no drama. Riff also shows relevant theoretical context in real time: what scale this riff uses, what chord is underneath, why this note works here.

Every session ends by playing a fragment of the target song. The project always visible, never abstract.

When you finish, Riff generates a summary: what went well, what error pattern keeps appearing, the theory behind the mistake, and a concrete exercise composed for the next session. The summary is skippable.

## What Riff does underneath

**Invisible spaced repetition.** Riff tracks when you last worked on each chord and technique. It reintroduces them silently just before you forget them, without announcing it.

**Automatic optimal difficulty.** It monitors your error rate in real time and adjusts without telling you: if you're failing too much it simplifies, if you're barely failing it raises the bar. The goal is to keep you always in the zone where you learn fastest — neither frustrated nor bored.

**Personal style engine.** Over time, Riff learns which genres you complete, which songs you abandon, which ones you repeat by your own initiative. That profile refines the plan and the generated exercises so they sound increasingly like music you actually like.

**Silent recording.** Riff saves representative fragments of your sessions without announcing it. After a month you can listen to your before and after. There is no more motivating metric than hearing yourself improve with your own ears.

**Daily discovery.** At the end of each session, as a reward, Riff presents something unexpected: a musical curiosity, a piece of rock history, or a small challenge connected to what you just played.

---

## Theory integrated, not separated

Riff has no theory lessons. Theory appears while you play — in the fixed line below the tab, in the briefing, in the post-session summary. And when you want to go deeper on something specific, you can ask directly: musical concepts, guitar techniques, historical context. It responds calibrated to your current level, without you having to specify it.

---

## What Riff is not

Riff is not a teacher. It doesn't lecture, it doesn't interrupt you while you're playing, it doesn't speak when it has nothing useful to say. It is a mentor: it knows more than you, but it trusts you'll get there. It puts you to play and watches. It adjusts when needed.

Riff is not a method built on sacrifice or discipline. It is built so that playing guitar is so enjoyable you can't stop.
