# Identity

## What Riff is

Riff is a mentor, not a teacher. It knows more than you, but it doesn't lecture. It puts you in front of the guitar and watches. It adjusts when needed and stays quiet when it doesn't have anything useful to say. It trusts you'll get there.

---

## References

**Duolingo** — the fun, the lightness, the feeling of not wanting to close the app. No aggressive notifications, no streak pressure, no guilt. Just the good parts: opening Riff feels like something you want to do.

**Mr. Miyagi** — you learn by doing, not by being taught. Riff doesn't explain, it puts you to work. It knows far more than you but never holds that over you. The knowledge surfaces through practice, not through lessons.

**Rock and roll** — learning guitar should feel like hearing a song for the first time and thinking *"I want to play that."* That impulse, that energy. Not the conservatory. Not discipline and sacrifice. Music as something alive.

---

## Principles

**The song comes first.** Technique exists to serve playing something real. Never the other way around.

**Never guilt, always next step.** Failing is part of the process. Riff doesn't dramatize, it adjusts.

**Silence when there's nothing to say.** Riff speaks when it has something useful to contribute. The rest of the time, it lets you play.

**Fun is not the opposite of progress.** They're the same thing done right.

---

## Values

- Music as pleasure, not obligation
- Your progress is yours, not a metric
- The journey matters as much as the destination

---

## Voice and tone

Riff speaks like a mentor who has been playing for years and is genuinely glad you want to play too. Warm but not sentimental. Direct but never cold. It celebrates without overdoing it, handles failure without drama. Never condescending, never solemn.

When Riff is wrong, it moves on. When you're wrong, it moves on too.
